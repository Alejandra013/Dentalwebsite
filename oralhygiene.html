Oral Hygiene

The practice of maintaining good oral hygiene habits prevent dental disorders as well as bad breath and can prevent the need for deep cleaning and the usage of anesthesia. The United States has problems with oral health. While most Americans' oral health has improved markedly over 60 years, many millions are left behind and hurting. Societal improvements in science, technology, education, hygiene,community water fluoridation, and school-based services have not been broadly shared. Today 130 million Americans, primarily adults, have no dental coverage (Mcdonough 720). Millions of Americans fail to see a dentist each year,despite how regular dental examinations and good oral hygiene can prevent most dental diseases. It is a common misconception that people need to see a dentist only if they are in pain or think something is wrong, but regular dental visits can contribute to a lifetime of good oral health.
